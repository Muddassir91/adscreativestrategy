Where You Are
02

You start at the output.

Brief lands. You open a doc. You write. If it flops, you rewrite. Same thinking, different words. There's nothing between the brief and the blank page. No research. No data. No "why should this work?" Just vibes and vocabulary.

What your answers told us

You skipped research. You jumped to headlines. When the data said something was broken, you guessed instead of diagnosed. When we asked for a brief, you wrote a script. That's the pattern.

The gaps in your game right now

  • No research step. You go from brief to writing with nothing in between. No Amazon reviews. No Reddit deep dives. No pulling competitor ads. The stuff that makes copy actually convert? Not in your process.
  • Headlines, not angles. You think in terms of "what sounds good." An angle is a strategic frame: shame, fear, aspiration, exhaustion. A headline is just one expression of that frame. You're picking outfits before you've picked the character.
  • No hypothesis. You don't decide WHY something should work before you make it. "I believe this angle hits because the data says X" vs. "this feels right." One is strategy. The other is a coin flip.
  • Can't read the numbers. We showed you performance data and you saw the problem but couldn't trace it to the root cause. The numbers tell a story. Right now you're looking at the cover and guessing the plot.
  • Briefs that are scripts. We asked for a creative brief. You gave us dialogue. A brief has an insight, a hypothesis, a test variable. A script has "Hi guys!" and a discount code. Big difference.

The 5 Core Skills
04

The 5 things that separate writers from strategists.

Creative strategy isn't one skill. It's five, working together. Here's each one, what it looks like in practice, and where you are on it. Honest version.

Not started

1. Research

Going to Amazon, Reddit, forums, competitor ads and pulling the actual words your audience uses. What they love. What they hate. What keeps them up at night. You skip this entirely right now.

Not started

2. Data Analysis

Looking at CTR, CPA, hook rate and figuring out what actually broke. Not "the ad flopped" but "the hook pulled in window-shoppers instead of buyers, that's why clicks were high but nobody purchased." You see the numbers. You can't read them yet.

Not started

3. Creative Briefing

Taking everything you learned from research and turning it into a document that tells the creative team what to make and why. The insight, the bet you're placing, the hook direction, what you're testing. You hand people scripts right now. That's a different thing.

Not started

4. Hypothesis Thinking

Before you make anything, you decide what bet you're placing. "I think this angle will hit this audience because the research showed X." Every ad is an experiment. If you don't know what you're testing, the result teaches you nothing. You're not placing bets yet. You're guessing.

Emerging

5. Iteration

When an ad dies, do you change the hook or kill the whole concept? Do you know the difference between the audience being tired of the LOOK vs. tired of the MESSAGE? You've got gut instincts here. But gut without a framework is just rolling dice with more confidence.
